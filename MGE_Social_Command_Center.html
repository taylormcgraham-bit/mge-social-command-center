Audience Health Score — Methodology
A composite 0–100 score blending two equally weighted pillars (50/50). All engagement metrics are normalized per post, so platforms with fewer posts in the selected date range aren't penalized for lower volume — only the quality of engagement on each post matters. Platforms with fewer than 2 posts in the period are excluded entirely and labeled “Low activity.” The overall gauge is a follower-weighted average of qualifying per-platform scores.

Engagement Quality (50%)

Interactions are scored per post using a tiered weighting system that values depth of engagement:

Tier 1 — Highest Value: Comments (weighted 5x) — indicate real conversation and audience investment.

Tier 2 — High Value: Shares & Saves (weighted 3x) — audience actively amplifying your content or bookmarking it for later.

Tier 3 — Moderate Value: Reactions/Likes (weighted 1x) — positive signal but low-effort interaction. Negative reactions (sad, angry, dislikes) are subtracted.

Tier 4 — Baseline: Views (weighted 0.2x) — passive consumption, valued as reach awareness.

The weighted total is divided by the number of posts in the period, then normalized via tanh curve (baseline: ~30 weighted engagements/post = score 50).

Comment Sentiment (50%)

Analyzes the tone of audience conversations using keyword-based sentiment scoring.

Confidence Weighting: The sentiment score is confidence-weighted by comment volume. With fewer than 15 comments, the score pulls toward neutral (50) to avoid over-reacting to small samples. With 0 comments, sentiment returns null and the algorithm gracefully degrades to engagement-only scoring.

Formula: final = (raw_sentiment × confidence) + (50 × (1 − confidence)), where confidence = min(1, comment_count / 15).

Minimum Post Threshold: Platforms with fewer than 2 posts in the selected date range are excluded from the overall score entirely. This prevents low-volume platforms (e.g., Instagram or YouTube during quiet periods) from dragging down the aggregate score when there simply isn’t enough data to evaluate.

Null-safe rescaling: If a pillar returns null (no comments) or a platform is excluded (low posts), the remaining data rescales automatically. The score reflects only platforms and pillars with meaningful activity.
